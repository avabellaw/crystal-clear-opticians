Our Services

Pre-screening

Auto-Refractor

Whilst focusing on this image, this machine will measure how well your eyes focus on the image. It will blur and unblur.

ⓘ

This test is to assist your optometrist by estimating your prescription (or RX for shorthand!).

Tonometer

You will see a blinking dot and 4 puffs of air will be used to determine the pressure of your eye.

ⓘ

People with high intraocular pressure are more likely to develop glaucoma.

Visual Fields Test

This will test how sensitive your visual field is and where there are blind spots.

There are 4 red dots, fixate on the center of these dots for the entirety of the test. You will also be handed a trigger with a button on top.
A white dot, with a brightness calibrated to the sensitivity of your eye, will appear in different parts of your visual field. Click the button everytime you see it.

This test will discover or monitor any array of conidition including but not limited to:

  • Glaucoma
  • Macular degeneration
  • Scotoma - Blind spot
  • Cataracts
ⓘ

This test can even detect the presence of a brain tumour!

Now you will see the optometrist who will determine your prescripion.

Your eye exam

After pre-screening, you meet you're optometrist.
What happens now?

One of the many tools at their disposal will be the Phoropter.

Phoropter

This will be used to find the perfect lens and help determine your final prescription.

Using the Auto-Refractor's results as baseline for your prescripion, the optometrist will ask you many questions and take varias measurements.

Some countries will rely on this machine alone but your ocular health is much more important than an estimate!

Cataracts

What is cataracts?

Most cataracts develops as you get older. It is when the lens of your eye thickens and becomes cloudy which makes seeing out of the affected eye(s) increasingly difficult.

Luckily, surgery has a 99% success rate and you'll be back on your feet within 2-6 weeks.

What is cataract-surgery?

Your cloudy lens will be removed and replaced with a clear plastic lens.

Refferal for surgery

We can discover cataracts during an eye test and refer you for surgery.
We will also give you advise on what to do next.

Post-operative appointment

After surgery, it's advised you book an appointment to evaluate your eye(s).

You will arrive 25 minutes before your appointment to have drops put in your eye(s) that will dilate your pupil(s)
This will enable the optometrist to see to the back of your eye clearly.

* You cannot drive after receiving eye drops that dilate pupils.

We offer transporation options for getting to and from the hospital or Crystal Clear Opticians.
